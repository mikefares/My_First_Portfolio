Introduction

Growing up in Ghana, a country in the western part of Africa, I was always fascinated by technology. The idea of self-driving cars, simple robots, and the exciting worlds created by virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) captivated my imagination. The workings of computers and the potential to transform dreams into digital realities inspired me to complete my undergraduate studies with a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering at Ghana Communication Technology University, Accra in 2021. I dream of one day contributing to the world of tech by blending my African heritage with the innovations and advancements of the modern world.

About Me

An experience that cemented my dedication to higher education was working on my final year project, which focused on using Face Recognition and Object Detection in schools to detect intruders. The technology aims to enhance security measures by identifying possible threats like guns or other weapons in images captured by cameras. This endeavor was influenced by the tragic events of the Boku Haram kidnappings of school children in Nigeria. It fueled my resolve to develop a system to enhance school security and create safer educational environments for students.
